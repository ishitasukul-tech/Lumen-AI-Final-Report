Executive Summary

LUMEN AI addresses one of the most persistent failures in K-12 literacy: students can often decode words without truly understanding what they are reading, and teachers rarely get continuous evidence of that gap while reading is happening. Existing tools usually assess before or after reading. LUMEN moves the measurement layer into the reading moment itself.

Our solution combines a camera-enabled reading wand, OCR, Lexile-aware question generation, conversational AI support, and a mobile analytics layer that tracks comprehension checkpoints and reading growth over time. Instead of waiting for a unit test or periodic benchmark, LUMEN creates a continuous comprehension loop: scan text, interpret the passage, ask adaptive questions, score responses, and surface actionable insights.

Why This Matters Now

  • NAEP 2024 found fewer than one-third of students are proficient readers at both grade 4 and grade 8.
  • About 40% of 4th graders scored below NAEP Basic in reading, the largest share since 2002.
  • About one-third of 8th graders scored below NAEP Basic, the highest share on record.
  • Only 14% of 13-year-olds said they read for fun almost daily, while 42% of lower-performing students said they never or hardly ever do.
  • The U.S. serves 49.6 million public-school students, 4.7 million private-school students, and roughly 3.8 million public-school teachers, creating both urgency and scale.

What Problem Are We Solving?

Reading instruction is often measured in snapshots, not streams. A teacher may know whether a student passed a quiz, but not where comprehension began to break down across a chapter, paragraph, or concept. In a class of 20 to 30 or more students, real-time one-on-one diagnosis is rarely possible. That means comprehension struggles can compound silently.

The problem is not just literacy access. It is the absence of continuous, quantitative comprehension data. Students need support at the exact moment confusion appears. Teachers need more than final scores; they need a sequence of evidence that shows how understanding changes over time and how reading level, question accuracy, and text complexity interact.

How LUMEN AI Solves It

  • Capture: The reading wand uses an onboard camera and lighting system to extract printed text in real time.
  • Interpret: OCR and preprocessing convert the visible passage into structured text.
  • Assess: LUMEN generates adaptive checkpoint questions tied to reading comprehension rather than answer-giving.
  • Support: Conversational AI gives level-appropriate explanations when students need help.
  • Quantify: The mobile app tracks Lexile-aligned progress, checkpoint accuracy, and trend data over time.
  • Inform: Teachers and parents receive timely insight into where students are on track and where they need intervention.

Why LUMEN Is Better Than the Competitors

Raz-Plus Benchmark passages, retellings, and comprehension quick checks that monitor reading progress over time. LUMEN inserts adaptive checkpoints during reading, not only before or after benchmark assignments.

Timeline: What We Built This Term

The timeline below is based directly on the team’s weekly LUMEN project updates and shows how the idea progressed from problem validation to prototype refinement and showcase preparation.

Week Focus What the team accomplished
Week 1 Problem Identification Clarified the core challenge: early reading comprehension is hard to monitor at scale, teacher bandwidth is limited, and the problem is significant across K-12 classrooms.
Week 2 Solution Design Sketched the LUMEN reading wand workflow, checkpoint logic, mobile app flow, and teacher dashboard path while ensuring AI would support thinking rather than replace it.
Week 3 Hardware Research and Design Selected key hardware components, designed the first mechanical layout, created a digital model, and 3D printed the initial prototype.
Week 4 OCR Integration and Device Refinement Investigated OCR model tradeoffs, storage needs, and camera options while revising the wand design to fit the image-to-text pipeline.
Week 5 Active Prototyping and Component Integration Ordered and assembled core components, evaluated OCR models against size and performance limits, and completed the first image-capture-to-structured-text stage.
Week 6 End-to-End Pipeline Testing Connected image capture, OCR, and language-model checkpoint generation into an integrated flow, exposing reliability issues that shaped the next debugging cycle.
Week 7 Pipeline Debugging and Prompt Refinement Worked through OCR setup problems, image-processing bugs, and language-model handoff issues; also drafted a more exact system prompt for comprehension support.
Week 8 OCR Reliability Improvements Improved lighting control, contrast enhancement, image cropping, and text formatting to increase OCR reliability before language-model processing.
Week 9 System Robustness and Complex Input Testing Stress-tested the system on imperfect OCR outputs and longer passages, improved prompt robustness, and added stronger logging to isolate failure points.
Week 10 Advanced Robustness Extended testing to multi-page documents, dense paragraphs, mixed fonts, and tables while continuing to strengthen preprocessing, lighting controls, and automated error tracking.
Week 11 I2P Showcase Preparation Polished the product experience, improved usability, and practiced the team pitch to communicate LUMEN’s value, purpose, and differentiation clearly.
Week 12 Final Showcase Preparation Refined the device and app, expanded the mobile database with more student data, improved interface clarity, and sharpened the data-backed explanation for judges.

Go-to-Market Logic

  • Phase 1: pilot LUMEN in classrooms where teachers already use reading groups and intervention blocks.
  • Phase 2: convert pilot success into school-level contracts built around measurable comprehension-growth reporting.
  • Phase 3: expand to district-level adoption by proving device reliability, teacher usability, and quantifiable student outcomes.
  • Parallel family market: offer a lower-friction app-plus-subscription pathway for at-home reading support.

Next Steps

  • Run a small classroom pilot to compare checkpoint accuracy, time-to-intervention, and Lexile growth against current practice.
  • Measure learning impact longitudinally by tracking weekly comprehension trends, not just single-assessment outcomes.
  • Harden the OCR pipeline for noisier inputs, longer books, and more diverse page layouts.
  • Build teacher dashboards that convert raw checkpoint data into intervention recommendations.
  • Strengthen privacy, student-safety, and AI-governance controls for deployment with minors.
  • Integrate with LMS ecosystems and classroom workflows so LUMEN fits existing school infrastructure.

Closing Argument

LUMEN AI is compelling because it does not treat reading comprehension as a black box. It transforms comprehension from an occasional outcome into a continuous signal. This matters for students who fall through the cracks, for teachers who cannot manually monitor every reading moment, and for schools that need better literacy data without adding more friction to the classroom.

Over this term, our team moved from problem discovery to hardware prototyping, OCR integration, AI checkpoint design, app analytics, and showcase readiness. The result is not just a concept. It is a working prototype and a clear product thesis: if reading comprehension can be measured continuously, it can be improved continuously. LUMEN AI is our answer to that opportunity.
